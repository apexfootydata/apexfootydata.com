Privacy

Last updated: 7 July 2026

The short version

FPL Apex has no accounts and no sign-ups. We don't sell, share, or monetise personal data. Almost everything stays in your browser; the one small exception is described below, so you know exactly what leaves your device.

Your FPL Team ID

When you enter your FPL Team ID, your squad is fetched directly from the official Fantasy Premier League API in your browser (via a proxy that adds no tracking). Your team and the plans you build are processed on your device and saved in your own browser's local storage. Clearing your browser data removes them. Note that FPL team information (team name, points, rank) is already public via the official FPL API for anyone who knows a Team ID.

Expected-minutes sync (the one thing we store)

If you edit a player's expected minutes, that single edit is saved to our hosting provider (Cloudflare) so it can follow you between devices. We store only: your FPL Team ID, the gameweek, and the minute values you set — nothing else, no name, no email, no payment data. These records expire automatically after about 45 days. An FPL Team ID is public but can be linked back to a person via the FPL API, so we treat it as personal data; our basis for holding it is that it's needed to deliver a feature you chose to use. To have yours removed, email us (below).

Analytics

We use Cloudflare Web Analytics and Google Analytics 4 to measure traffic — pages visited, rough location, device type. This tells us which tools people use; it isn't tied to your FPL identity. Where consent is required, it's collected on first visit.

Cookies & local storage

Beyond the analytics above, the site uses your browser's local storage for convenience only: remembering your Team ID and saved transfer plans between visits, on your device.

Questions or deletion requests

Email contact@fplapex.com. Most of your data lives only in your browser, so "deletion" is usually just clearing your browser storage. For the expected-minutes records described above, tell us your Team ID and we'll delete them (they also expire on their own after ~45 days).
